## Introduction

The coffee shop industry has become highly competitive, making it essential for businesses to understand customer purchasing patterns and sales trends. Data analytics helps coffee shop managers identify peak sales periods, popular products, customer preferences, and revenue drivers for effective decision-making.

The analysis was carried out using Excel's data analysis and power bi to develop an interactive dashboard that provides clear and actionable insights into the coffee shop's operations.



## Research Objectives

The objectives are to:
1.  Analyze the overall sales performance of the coffee shop. 
2.  Examine monthly sales trends. 
3. Determine the best-performing store location. 
4. Identify the most profitable product categories. 
5.  Analyze daily sales patterns. 
6.  Examine hourly sales trends. 
7. Examine the trends of product categories in store location
8. Provide recommendations for improving business performance. 
 

## Research Questions

1.  What is the overall sales performance of the coffee shop? Which products are sold  most and least?
2.  How do sales vary across different months? 
3.  Which store location generates the highest revenue? 
4.  Which product categories contribute most to sales? 
5.  Which days generate the highest revenue? 
6.  At what times of the day do customers make the most purchases? 
7  How do store locations affect product category?
8   What strategies can improve coffee shop sales? 


## Analysis and Findings

## 1. What is the overall sales performance of the coffee shop? Which products are sold  most and least?

### Method

A Pivot Table was used to calculate the sum of all sales values, and the result was displayed as a KPI Card on the dashboard.Also a column chart was used to indicate the product that sold most.

### Finding

   The coffee shop generated a total revenue of $698812.33 from 149116 transactions. Barista Espresso sold most with 16403 transactions while Green beans sold the least with 134 transactions.

## 2. How do sales vary across different months?

### Method

A Pivot Table was used to count the total number of transaction quantity across different months, which was displayed as a KPI Card. A bar chart was used show the trends.

### Finding

Sales generally increased over the study period although February experienced a slight decline from January, sales rose consistently from March to June. June recorded the highest sales. This shows the demand is increasing and as it increases, there is growth in the business.

## 3. Which store location generates the highest revenue? 

### Method

A Pivot Table was used to sum the sales made by the locations, and the percentages were indicated on the doughnut chart.

### Finding

Hell's kitchen generated the highest revenue, though the difference among the three stores was relatively small.  This indicates a balanced performance across locations.

## 4. Which product categories contribute most to sales?

### Method

The sum function in a Pivot Table was used to calculate the sum of differnt product category, which was intrepreted using column chart.

### Finding

Coffee contributed the highest sales followed by tea then bakery.

## 5.Which days generate the highest revenue? 

### Method

A Pivot Table summarized revenue by days, while a line chart was used to rank days from the highest to the lowest revenue.

### Finding

Sales remained relatively stable throughout the week. Mondays and Fridays showed slightly stronger performance, while Saturdays had the lowest revenue.

## 6. At what times of the day do customers make the most purchases? 

### Method

Sales revenue by time using a Pivot Table and visualized with a line chart.

### Finding

Sales was at its peak between  8 and 10 in the morning, this reflects breakfast purchase and workday  commuting patterns. Demand  reduces after 10am

## 7. How do store locations affect product category?

### Method

Count of transaction quantity in different stores were summarized using a Pivot Table and displayed with a clustered column chart to visualize sales trends over time.

### Finding

Sales differed substantially among product categories and  average sales were similar across Astoria, Hell's Kitchen, and Lower Manhattan. Therefore, product category, rather than store location, appears to be the primary factor influencing sales performance.

## 8. What strategies can improve coffee shop sales?

### Method

Recommendations were developed based on insights obtained from the dashboard analysis.

### Finding

The analysis suggests the following strategies:

- Increase inventory for high-performing products.
- Introduce promotional campaigns for low-performing products.
- Bundle complementary products to increase average customer spending.
- Monitor monthly sales trends to support business planning.
- Use dashboard insights for continuous performance monitoring and decision-making.

## Summary

This project analyzed coffee shop sales data using Microsoft Excel to evaluate business performance and identify key sales trends. An interactive dashboard was developed to monitor essential Key Performance Indicators (KPIs), including **Total Revenue**, **Transaction quantity** and **Average Sales**.

The analysis also examined product performance, product category performance, and monthly sales trends to better understand customer purchasing behavior and overall business performance. Using Pivot Tables, Pivot Charts, KPI Cards, and Slicers, the dashboard provides an interactive and user-friendly way to explore the data and support business decision-making.

Overall, the project demonstrates how Excel can be used to transform raw sales data into meaningful insights that help businesses monitor performance, identify opportunities, and make data-driven decisions.


## Recommendations

Based on the findings from the analysis, the following recommendations are proposed:

### 1. Focus on High-Performing Products
Maintain adequate inventory for products that consistently generate high revenue and consider featuring them in promotional campaigns.

### 2. Improve Sales of Low-Performing Products
Introduce discounts, product bundles, or targeted marketing campaigns to increase the sales of products with lower demand.

### 3. Optimize Product Categories
Allocate more resources to high-performing product categories while reviewing the pricing, placement, or product mix of lower-performing categories.

### 4. Use Sales Trends for Business Planning
Leverage monthly sales trends to forecast demand, plan inventory levels, and schedule staff efficiently during peak periods.

### 5. Monitor Business Performance Regularly
Continue using the interactive dashboard to track KPIs and identify changes in sales performance, allowing for timely and informed business decisions.

### 6. Strengthen Data-Driven Decision Making
Update the dashboard regularly with new sales data to ensure management decisions are based on current and accurate information.
